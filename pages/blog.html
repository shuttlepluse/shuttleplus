The Ultimate Guide to Addis Ababa: Top 20 Things to Do

From ancient churches to vibrant markets, world-class museums to stunning viewpoints - discover everything the "New Flower" of Africa has to offer visitors.

Historical Landmarks

Explore Addis Ababa's rich history through its iconic monuments and sites

Landmarks

Holy Trinity Cathedral

Ethiopia's most important cathedral and final resting place of Emperor Haile Selassie. Marvel at stunning stained glass windows and intricate architecture blending Ethiopian and European styles.

Arat Kilo 25 min from airport
Visit early morning for peaceful atmosphere and best photos
Landmarks

Meskel Square

The heart of Addis Ababa - this massive public square hosts the famous Meskel Festival, political gatherings, and serves as the city's main meeting point. A must-see for understanding Ethiopian culture.

City Center 20 min from airport
Visit during Meskel Festival (September) for an unforgettable experience
Landmarks

National Palace (Jubilee Palace)

The official residence of Ethiopian heads of state since Emperor Haile Selassie. While the interior isn't open to public, the grounds and exterior architecture are impressive from outside.

Arat Kilo 25 min from airport
Combine with nearby Holy Trinity Cathedral visit
Landmarks

African Union Headquarters

The iconic AU building, gifted by China, dominates the skyline. The conference center and grounds symbolize pan-African unity. Visit the nearby monument celebrating African independence.

Kazanchis 15 min from airport
Best viewed at night when illuminated

Museums & Culture

Dive deep into Ethiopia's 3,000+ year history and diverse cultures

Culture

National Museum of Ethiopia

Home to "Lucy" - the 3.2 million year old hominid fossil that changed our understanding of human evolution. Also features Ethiopian art, royal artifacts, and historical treasures.

King George VI St 20 min from airport
Allow 2-3 hours. Lucy replica is upstairs - don't miss it!
Culture

Ethnological Museum

Located in Emperor Haile Selassie's former palace on Addis Ababa University campus. Explore Ethiopia's 80+ ethnic groups through traditional artifacts, clothing, and customs.

Sidist Kilo 25 min from airport
Don't miss the emperor's bedroom and bathroom - preserved as he left them
Culture

Red Terror Martyrs Memorial Museum

A sobering but important museum documenting the atrocities of the Derg regime (1974-1991). Features personal belongings, photographs, and testimonies of victims.

Meskel Square 20 min from airport
Free entry. Emotionally heavy - allow time to reflect
Culture

Zoma Museum

A contemporary art space and sustainable architecture showcase. Features rotating exhibitions, beautiful gardens, and an organic restaurant. Perfect blend of art and nature.

Mekanisa 30 min from airport
Book a guided tour to fully appreciate the architecture

Nature & Outdoors

Escape the city buzz with these natural retreats and scenic spots

Nature

Entoto Mountain & Park

At 3,000m elevation, Entoto offers panoramic views of Addis Ababa. Explore eucalyptus forests, visit ancient churches, and watch women carrying firewood - a scene unchanged for centuries.

North Addis 35 min from airport
Bring a jacket - it's noticeably cooler at this altitude
Nature

Addis Ababa Zoo (Lion Zoo)

Home to the rare Ethiopian lion with its distinctive black mane. The zoo focuses on native Ethiopian wildlife conservation including endemic species.

Sidist Kilo 20 min from airport
Best time to see active lions is early morning
Nature

Unity Park

A beautifully renovated palace grounds featuring gardens, a zoo with Ethiopian wildlife, historical exhibits, and traditional houses representing Ethiopia's regions.

Arat Kilo 25 min from airport
Book tickets online to avoid queues. Allow half a day
Nature

Gefersa Reservoir

A peaceful escape just outside the city. The reservoir and surrounding area offer birdwatching opportunities and pleasant walks with mountain views.

West Addis 45 min from airport
Perfect for a morning trip combined with Entoto visit

Markets & Shopping

From Africa's largest market to artisan crafts - find unique treasures

Culture

Merkato Market

Africa's largest open-air market is an overwhelming sensory experience. Miles of stalls selling everything from spices to electronics, traditional clothing to recycled goods.

Merkato 30 min from airport
Go with a guide, leave valuables at hotel, and bargain hard!
Culture

Shiro Meda (Fabric Market)

The best place to buy traditional Ethiopian clothing and fabrics. Find beautiful handwoven cotton, traditional dresses, and have custom clothing made.

Shiro Meda 25 min from airport
Visit morning for best selection. Custom orders take 1-3 days
Culture

Entoto Market

A quieter alternative to Merkato, located near Entoto Mountain. Great for handicrafts, traditional items, and a more relaxed shopping experience.

Entoto 35 min from airport
Combine with Entoto Mountain visit
Culture

Tomoca Coffee

Ethiopia is the birthplace of coffee, and Tomoca is legendary. This tiny shop has been roasting and selling premium Ethiopian coffee since 1953. Buy beans to take home.

Piazza 25 min from airport
Order a macchiato standing at the bar like locals do

Food & Dining

Experience Ethiopia's unique and delicious cuisine

Food

Traditional Ethiopian Food Guide

Injera, doro wat, kitfo, tibs - learn about Ethiopia's unique cuisine. Everything is served on injera (sourdough flatbread) and eaten with your hands. It's a communal, social experience.

Must-try dishes
Always eat with your right hand. Gursha (feeding others) is a sign of respect
Food

Yod Abyssinia Cultural Restaurant

The ultimate Ethiopian dining experience. Traditional food served with live music, dance performances showcasing Ethiopia's diverse cultures, and coffee ceremony.

Bole 10 min from airport
Reserve in advance, especially for dinner shows
Food

Ethiopian Coffee Ceremony

Experience the birthplace of coffee through a traditional ceremony. Green beans are roasted, ground, and brewed before you in an aromatic ritual that's been practiced for centuries.

Allow 1-2 hours
Three cups is traditional - buna (1st), tona (2nd), baraka (3rd, blessing)
Food

Local Tej Houses

Try tej - Ethiopian honey wine served in distinctive flask-shaped glasses (berele). Tej houses are traditional establishments where locals gather for drinks and conversation.

Traditional drink
Tej is deceptively sweet but quite strong - pace yourself!

Travel Tips

Essential information for visiting Addis Ababa

Best Time to Visit

October to May offers dry weather and comfortable temperatures. September features Meskel Festival. Avoid heavy rains in July-August.

Weather & Altitude

At 2,400m elevation, Addis is cooler than expected. Expect 15-25Â°C year-round. Bring layers and allow time to adjust to the altitude.

Currency & Money

Ethiopian Birr (ETB) is the currency. ATMs are available but bring USD cash as backup. Credit cards accepted at major hotels and restaurants.

Ethiopian Calendar

Ethiopia uses a unique calendar - it's currently 7-8 years "behind" the Gregorian calendar. Don't be confused by dates!

Ethiopian Time

Ethiopians count hours from sunrise (6 AM = 12 o'clock). Always clarify if times are in "Ethiopian" or "European" time!

Getting Around

Book airport transfers in advance. Traffic can be chaotic. Ride-hailing apps exist but pre-arranged transfers are more reliable.
